Important information about Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes is a disease which inhibits the body's capability of producing insulin. As a result, an afflicted individual's blood sugar levels can go unregulated and cause many health issues.
The disease typically appears in adolescence.

Causes of Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes is an auto-immune disease characterized by the immune system attacking the body's insulin-producing cells.
Although the causes of Type 1 Diabetes are unknown, it is thought to be genetic in nature.

Symptoms of Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes demonstrates a variety of symptoms, all stemming from an uncontrolled blood sugar level.

Common symptoms include:
  • Shakiness
  • Nervousness/anxiety
  • Sweating/chills/clamminess
  • Irritability/impatience
  • Dizziness
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Hunger/nausea
  • Blurred vision
  • Weakness/fatigue

Treatments for Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes is a disease that cannot be cured. It, however, can be treated through close involvement of the afflicted individual and those around them.

Here is a list of effective methods of treatment:
  • Monitor your blood sugar levels
  • Use an insulin pump
  • Utilize traditional insulin injections
  • Adopt healthy eating habits
  • Incorporate more exercise into your life
  • Make regular appointments with your physician
