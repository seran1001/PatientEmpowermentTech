Important information about Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 Diabetes is a disease characterized by a resistence to insulin in the body.
The disease can manifest at any age, though it is the most common after the age of 45.

Causes of Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 Diabetes is a disease which is caused by a bodily resistence to the insulin produced by the pancreas. This leads to both an unregulated blood sugar level and the pancreas losing its ability to effectively produce insulin.

Symptoms of Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 Diabetes demonstrates a variety of symptoms, all stemming from an uncontrolled blood sugar level.

Common symptoms include:
  • Frequent urination
  • Increased thirst
  • Constant hunger
  • Slowed healing
  • Constant fatigue
  • Blurry vision
  • Patches of dark skin
  • Itching/yeast infection
  • Tingling/pain/numbness of the hands/feet
  • Anger/stubbornness/sadness

Treatments for Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 Diabetes is a disease that cannot be cured. It, however, can be treated through close involvement of the afflicted individual and those around them.

Here is a list of effective methods of treatment:
  • Monitor your blood sugar levels
  • Use an insulin pump
  • Utilize traditional insulin injections
  • Use medication to increase insulin production in the pancreas
  • Adopt healthy eating habits
  • Incorporate more exercise into your life
  • Make regular appointments with your physician
